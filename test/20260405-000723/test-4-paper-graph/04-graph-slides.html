Development Narrative

DETR (2020) established the end-to-end detection paradigm by replacing hand-crafted components (NMS, anchors) with a Transformer encoder-decoder and Hungarian matching. Its two main limitations were slow training convergence (500 epochs) and poor small-object performance, both rooted in the global attention mechanism's inability to efficiently handle high-resolution, multi-scale features.

Deformable DETR (2021) addressed both issues by introducing deformable attention that attends to only a sparse set of learned sampling points, enabling multi-scale feature fusion and reducing convergence time by 10x. This became the de facto backbone architecture for subsequent DETR variants.

By 2023, the research front split into two parallel efforts. DINO tackled precision through contrastive denoising training and improved query initialization, pushing DETR-family accuracy to 63.2 AP and achieving parity with the strongest conventional detectors. Co-DETR attacked the same underlying problem (sparse supervision from one-to-one matching) from a complementary angle: auxiliary one-to-many heads enrich encoder gradients during training while being discarded at inference, reaching 66.0 AP with additional data.

RT-DETR (2024) shifted focus to deployment efficiency. By synthesizing advances from Deformable DETR and DINO into a hybrid encoder with separated intra-scale and cross-scale processing, it achieved the first DETR model to beat YOLO-family detectors on the real-time speed-accuracy Pareto frontier, validating the practical viability of the end-to-end detection paradigm.
