### Logical Narrative

The field of vision-language detection originates from a single architectural insight in CLIP (2021): contrastive pretraining on web-scale image-text pairs produces an embedding space general enough to support zero-shot recognition. However, CLIP operates at the image level, leaving a representation gap when the task requires localizing individual objects within a scene. The first wave of methods in 2022 attacked this gap from three distinct angles. RegionCLIP addressed it by fine-tuning CLIP's encoder on region-text pairs generated via RPN proposals, learning region-level representations within the original CLIP space. OWL-ViT took a minimalist route, attaching small detection heads (classification + box regression) directly onto the frozen CLIP ViT backbone, showing that even simple architectural additions could yield competent zero-shot detection. GLIP, meanwhile, proposed a more radical integration: reformulating detection as phrase grounding and introducing deep cross-modal fusion during both feature extraction and prediction stages, achieving strong performance but at significant computational cost.

The period from 2023 to 2024 saw these threads mature and converge. DetCLIPv2 scaled RegionCLIP's region-text alignment paradigm to 13 million images, demonstrating that data scale and word-level (rather than phrase-level) alignment substantially improve novel-category generalization. Grounding DINO married GLIP's grounding paradigm with the DINO transformer detector, replacing GLIP's Dynamic Head with a transformer decoder that enables tighter cross-modal fusion across three stages (feature enhancement, language-guided query selection, cross-modality decoding). This combination proved effective: Grounding DINO became one of the most adopted open-set detectors. Its successor, Grounding DINO 1.5, further scaled the model to over 20 million training images and introduced an edge-optimized variant, advancing both accuracy (particularly on rare categories) and deployment practicality.

In parallel, YOLO-World demonstrated that real-time open-vocabulary detection is achievable by embedding CLIP-style text features into the YOLO architecture via a Re-parameterizable Vision-Language Path Aggregation Network. At 52 FPS with 35.4 AP on LVIS, it operates over an order of magnitude faster than Grounding DINO while maintaining competitive accuracy, highlighting a clear speed-accuracy trade-off axis in the field. CLIFF, presented at ECCV 2024, opened a conceptually different direction by formulating open-vocabulary detection through continual latent diffusion, treating the distribution transfer among object, image, and text latent spaces as a generative modeling problem rather than a discriminative one. This diversity of approaches suggests the field has not yet converged on a single dominant paradigm; instead, the design space spans discriminative grounding, contrastive region alignment, architectural re-parameterization, and generative diffusion, each offering distinct trade-offs in generalization, speed, and continual learning capacity.
